Chew on This: The Dynamics of Bite Force

The human masticatory system is an engineering marvel, seamlessly integrating biomechanics for the vital process of mastication. This presentation aims to dissect the complexities of force transmission during the chewing process and illuminate the effects of dental pathologies, such as caries, on this sophisticated system. Through a blend of biomechanical principles, we will explore how the intricate network of muscles, bones, and teeth collaborate to efficiently break down food, and how this synergy is compromised when dental caries enters the picture. Our journey begins with an overview of the masticatory system's anatomy and the biomechanical processes at work during normal chewing. We will then shift our focus to the pathophysiology of dental caries, investigating how the structural integrity of a tooth is undermined by decay. Integrating insights from recent research, we will examine how a carious lesion alters the distribution of masticatory forces, potentially leading to altered chewing patterns, compensatory mechanisms, and additional stress on surrounding teeth and tissues. The presentation will also highlight the broader implications of compromised mastication, including its impact on nutrition and systemic health. By the end of our session, attendees will have gained a comprehensive understanding of the masticatory system's biomechanics, the significant influence of dental caries on force transmission, and the importance of maintaining oral health for the systemic well-being of the human body. Join us as we chew over the details of this critical aspect of human biomechanics.
